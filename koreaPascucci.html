  • 130 years of authentic Italian espressocoffee shop CAFFE PASCUCCICentral Italy - a natural environment for green coffee storage Since green beans grown and processed in a production area tens of thousands of kilometers away from Italy and transported by ship retain the salt and moisture of the sea during the transportation period, the quality can be maintained continuously through the control and management of humidity and temperature before roasting. have to let it happen.

    'Monte Cerignone' in central Italy is a place where the Pascucci family has kept green beans for a long time, and it is a place like a natural fortress where constant temperature and low humidity are maintained throughout the year. The micro- climate of 600m in the valley of the Conga River allows green beans to maintain their unique flavor and naturally mature over time, so it is deeper than the state-of -the-art green bean storage warehouse and is independent of the climate and crop conditions of the producing area. This allows for a consistent coffee taste.

  • Representative of Italian coffee culture -blending of thePascucci family A total of 9 types of green coffee beans are blended under the ratio of 80% Arabica and 20% Robusta through expert testing. The reason why the Pascucci family uses Robusta for blending comes from Italian coffee culture, the home of espresso. In Italy, there has long been a preference for blending Robusta with Arabica to give espresso its heavy body and deep bitterness. The blending know- how of the Pascucci family is to express the aromatic and heavy body of Robusta with the acidity and rich taste and aroma of Arabica. Also, due to the use of quality Robusta species, stable extraction and unique golden crema can be enjoyed for a long time.

  • Slow roasting to bring out the unique taste and flavor of green beans When blending, it is important to roast evenly, so unique know-how is required. Pascucci does slow roasting of well-blended blended green beans with medium roasting. Slow roasting is a method of slowly roasting at medium heat for 15 to 18 minutes, which is longer than the normal roasting time of 11 to 14 minutes, to naturally evaporate the moisture in green beans and to bring out the good taste and flavor components inside. no see. Slow roasting, which is optimized for blending of Pascucci, is one of the most important processes to awaken the unique body and bitter taste of espresso with less burnt taste.

  • Foundation for connecting tradition and authenticity - 130 years of espresso research After the Pascucci family started trading green beans in 1883 and established a roasting factory that served as a stepping stone for the spread of the coffee industry, research on Italian espresso began actively. At first, it was simply a study on green coffee beans, but later, in order to strengthen the quality of espresso, we established our own research institute operated by a group of experts such as agronomists and scientists, and continued quality control at the production site through history of each green coffee bean, standard management of defective coffee beans, and research on mold and pests. It has expanded into research that enables the production of good green coffee beans. Through this, we provide various information to producers with poor research environments and support them to maintain sustainable quality. Pascucci is able to maintain the unchanging taste of espresso for 130 years through continuous research and sharing of information, and is creating a sustainable coffee industry with the production area.

  • For the taste and experience of Italian tradition, premium coffee beans - golden color Golden Sack for realizing authentic Italian coffee from Pascucci is a premium bean that has been verified through Italian Pascucci, internal researcher and consumer tests.The freshly extracted espresso with a golden sack has a medium body and an excellent balance between the acidity of dried fruits and the sweetness of milk chocolate. In addition, it has rich aromas of brown sugar, cookies, and toasted almonds, and a good aftertaste that lingers for a long time after a soft throat.

  • A barista's passion for the best cup of Italian espresso - 7 philosophies Coffee beans that have gone through all processes of green bean selection, aging, blending, and roasting can be enjoyed by baristas with a cup of espresso. Well- skilled baristas play an important role in finishing a cup of espresso, so they inherit the history and tradition of the Italian Pascucci family and brew with the following 7 philosophies and principles.

    1. 1) 1We do our best to be honest and sincere.
    2. 2) We work passionately for our customers and partners and never compromise on coffee quality.

    4. 4) Don't be afraid of challenges and keep trying.
